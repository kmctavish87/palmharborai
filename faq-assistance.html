FAQ Assistance

Answer repeat questions automatically while keeping a human handoff available.

A support assistant can handle common questions, gather context, and pass more complex issues to the right person.

← Back to Automations

Where it helps most

  • Hours, pricing, policy, and onboarding questions.
  • Pre-qualifying support issues before human review.
  • Collecting the details a support team needs up front.
  • Improving response speed outside business hours.

Outcome

Customers get faster answers, and support teams get fewer repetitive tickets.

Pre-sales questions

Answer common questions about services, pricing bands, and fit before the lead reaches a human.

Support deflection

Handle simple policy or onboarding questions automatically so staff can focus on real edge cases.

After-hours coverage

Give visitors helpful, immediate responses even when the team is offline, while collecting context for follow-up.

Recommended starting point

Launch with a narrow FAQ set and strong escalation rules instead of trying to answer everything on day one.

Helpful inputs

  • FAQ knowledge base
  • Business hours and policies
  • Escalation triggers
  • Hand-off form fields

Success metric

Look at first-response speed, deflected tickets, and whether escalated issues arrive with better context.

Strong fit

  • Repeat questions with stable answers
  • After-hours lead capture
  • Support teams with high FAQ volume
  • Businesses with a usable knowledge base

Not ideal yet

  • When answers change constantly
  • When there is no escalation path
  • When the knowledge base is outdated
  • When sensitive issues require immediate human judgment
