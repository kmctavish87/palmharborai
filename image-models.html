Image Models

Image models turn text ideas into visual concepts for marketing and design.

They are useful when a business needs mockups, concept art, or quick creative exploration before spending time on polished production.

← Back to Models

Common business uses

Image models are strongest when they speed up visual experimentation. They help teams test ideas before paying for final photography or design work.

  • Create draft ad visuals and social graphic concepts.
  • Mock up website hero images and promotional art.
  • Explore packaging, signage, or branding directions.
  • Generate internal concept boards for client discussions.

Best for

Early-stage visual ideation, campaign planning, and content testing.

Simple prompt“Create a bright beachside hero image for a Florida small business AI brand.”
Watch out forBrand consistency and unrealistic details. Use final human review before public launch.

Ad concepts

Generate several visual directions for a seasonal campaign before selecting one for polished production.

Website mockups

Quickly create placeholder hero imagery so a landing page can be designed before photography is ready.

Brand exploration

Test color palettes, scenes, and moods for a new brand direction without paying for every concept upfront.

Recommended starting point

Use image models for ideation first, then move final public visuals into a tighter design review process.

Prompt recipe

  • Describe subject, style, and mood.
  • Include brand colors or visual references.
  • Specify framing and aspect ratio.
  • Iterate in small steps.

Good fit

Best for concept work, campaign exploration, and situations where “draft visual” is enough to move a project forward.

Great use cases

  • Visual ideation
  • Mockups and concept boards
  • Campaign exploration
  • Creative direction testing

Use caution when

  • Brand details must be exact
  • Product realism matters heavily
  • Rights and licensing need review
  • Final design polish is required
