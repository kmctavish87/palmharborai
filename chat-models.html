Chat Models

Chat models help businesses write, answer, summarize, and think faster.

They are usually the easiest entry point for AI because they work through plain-language instructions and can support many different tasks.

← Back to Models

Where chat models fit best

Use them when the work starts with language: drafting emails, rewriting copy, summarizing notes, answering internal questions, or helping someone think through a task.

  • Create first drafts for blog posts, landing pages, and social content.
  • Turn messy notes into clean summaries or action items.
  • Help staff answer repeat questions with a consistent tone.
  • Act as an internal assistant for brainstorming and research.

Best for

Marketing, operations, support, and founder workflows where speed and clarity matter.

Simple prompt“Summarize these meeting notes into three decisions and five next steps.”
Watch out forOverconfident answers. Always review important outputs before publishing.

Marketing example

A local service business can use a chat model to draft blog posts, rewrite ad copy, and turn one offer into several social media variations.

Operations example

A team can paste messy meeting notes into a chat model and get a clean task list, owner list, and next-step summary in seconds.

Support example

Support staff can use a chat model to prepare better responses faster while still reviewing the final answer before sending it.

Recommended starting point

Start with low-risk internal writing and summarization before moving into customer-facing use cases.

Prompt recipe

  • Give the model a role.
  • Explain the audience and goal.
  • Define the output format clearly.
  • Add examples when quality matters.

Good fit

Use chat models when speed, clarity, and first-draft support matter more than perfect autonomy.
